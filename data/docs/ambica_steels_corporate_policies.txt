1. CORPORATE MISSION, VISION & CORE VALUES (5C FRAMEWORK)
===============================================================================

1.1 VISION STATEMENT
Ambica Steels Limited envisions becoming a global leader in the stainless steel 
industry, renowned for operational excellence, disruptive innovation, and high-
tier manufacturing capabilities. The organization aims to transform the long 
product steel landscape by defining structural industry benchmarks for quality, 
environmental sustainability, and client-centric engineering.

1.2 MISSION STATEMENT
To consistently manufacture and deliver superior-grade stainless steel products 
(including bright bars, angle bars, and fine wires) that systematically conform 
to global rigorous parameters and transcend stakeholder expectations. We pledge 
to cultivate a culture of unceasing internal process improvement by channeling 
capital into research, development, and advanced metallurgical technologies.

1.3 CORE VALUES: THE 5C FRAMEWORK
Our structural value ecosystem is anchored on five foundational pillars that 
govern cross-departmental operations, workforce expectations, and strategic B2B 
partnerships:

  A. CREDIBILITY: Trust is established and preserved daily via product 
     homogeneity, strict process reproducibility, and unwavering structural 
     integrity. For clients, this represents zero-defect product security.
  B. COLLABORATION: Synergistic ecosystem management involving internal business 
     units, global raw material vendors, and end consumers to generate long-term 
     shared value and seamlessly engineered supply chains.
  C. CURIOSITY: Active promotion of continuous technical exploration, encouraging 
     R&D teams to study emerging metallurgical variations, next-gen machinery, 
     and automation infrastructure to stay ahead of international market cycles.
  D. COMMUNICATION: Complete data transparency across operational verticals. 
     This ensures accurate customer batch tracking, explicit production state updates, 
     and flat-hierarchy dialogue internally.
  E. COMMITMENT: Persistent execution of service agreements, customer delivery schedules, 
     workforce safety protections, and specialized corporate social targets.

2. STANDARD TERMS & CONDITIONS OF SALES POLICY
===============================================================================

2.1 OFFER AND CONTRACT FORMATION
  - Form of Quotation: Written quotations issued by Ambica Steels Limited (ASL) 
    constitute a formal "Offer" which remains contractually valid for an exact 
    window of two (2) days from the formal date of issue, unless alternate terms 
    are clearly stated within the transaction text.
  - Revocation Rights: ASL retains unconditional legal authorization to revoke an 
    outstanding Offer at any phase prior to receiving the Buyer's formal written 
    acceptance by executing a written notice.
  - Final Binding Contract: A legally enforceable contract is explicitly formed 
    only when ASL evaluates, accepts, issues, and dispatches a formal "Sale Order" 
    document to the purchasing entity.
  - Assignment Restrictions: The Buyer is prohibited from assigning, transferring, 
    or offloading the contract or its associated benefits without the explicit 
    advance written consent of ASL management. Amendments or cancelations require 
    bilateral written approval.

2.2 TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
  - Goods delivered will conform strictly to the technical parameter sheets explicitly 
    detailed inside the finalized "Sale Order."
  - General product details listed inside advertising brochures, handbooks, 
    digital portals, or generic price sheets are completely non-binding unless 
    individually integrated into the Sale Order framework. No supplementary 
    implied or inferred warranties are recognized.

2.3 PRICE, TAXES, AND PAYMENT TERMS
  - Tax Exclusions: Quoted Offer Prices exclude Goods and Services Tax (GST), 
    customs clearing levies, import/export duties, or associated statutory fees.
  - Unconditional Payment Obligation: Buyers must remit payments strictly matching 
    the payment timeline clauses explicitly set out in the Sale Order. The Buyer 
    is legally barred from withholding, delaying, or offsetting payments under 
    any scenario, including active product quality disputes, volume shortages, 
    or operational arguments.
  - Overdue Interest Penalties: Delinquent invoices trigger an automatic compounding 
    interest penalty calculated at eighteen percent (18%) per annum, applied 
    from the date of initial default up to the final execution date of payment.

2.4 DELIVERY, LOGISTICS, AND LIABILITY
  - Best-Efforts Delivery Timeline: ASL commits reasonable structural maneuvers to 
    satisfy stated shipping timelines. However, if transit disruptions manifest due 
    to force majeure incidents or issues outside ASLâs control, the corporation 
    shall assume zero liability for direct, indirect, or collateral financial damages.
  - Lot Distribution: ASL retains full operational liberty to divide production volumes 
    and deliver commercial goods in staggered lots.
  - Default Freight Framework: Where precise freight definitions are omitted in the 
    contract documentation, Ex Works (Incoterms 2000) at an ASL facility of the 
    company's choice will serve as the governance baseline.
  - Failure to Take Delivery: If a Buyer fails to clear or claim the arriving goods 
    within one (1) week of delivery at the designated destination point, ASL 
    retains an unrestricted legal option to redirect or sell the cargo to 
    alternative clients. The original Buyer must issue an immediate, un-demurred 
    "No Objection Certificate" (NOC) and absorb all storage, demurrage, and diversion costs.

3. SKILLING AND MENTORSHIP POLICY (FOREIGN TRADE POLICY COMPLIANCE)
===============================================================================

3.1 REGULATORY ALIGNMENT
In strict accordance with the mandatory provisions governing Status Holders under 
the national Foreign Trade Policy issued by the Directorate General of Foreign 
Trade (DGFT), ASL operates an institutional framework to optimize technical 
skill distribution, industrial safety, and local economic development.

3.2 SKILLING INITIATIVES (INTERNAL & INDUSTRY INDUCTION)
  - Technical & Quality Alignment: Routine structured training cycles targeting 
    internal operators, engineers, and contractual technicians regarding advanced 
    metallurgical processing, precise machinery maintenance, and quality safety protocols.
  - Global Logistics Compliance: Tailored specialized courses detailing export 
    documentation standards, automated customs clearing, global shipping frameworks, 
    and dynamic regulatory compliance.
  - Educational Apprenticeships: Maintenance of systematic, structured internship, 
    apprenticeship, and industrial training tracks targeting engineering and vocational 
    students to cultivate the next wave of industrial human capital.

3.3 MENTORSHIP INITIATIVES (EXTERNAL MSME SUPPORT)
  - ASL extends corporate mentorship, infrastructure knowledge, and process insight 
    to Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs), industrial startups, and aspiring 
    exporters.
  - Core Mentorship Pillars: Sharing of industry practices regarding international trade compliance, 
    standardization requirements for western economies, precise quality matrix control, 
    and foreign business development strategies.

3.4 LOGISTICAL OPERATIONS & DATA RECORDING
  - Dissemination Formats: Programs may be delivered via physically located training modules, 
    centralized online instruction portals, interactive industrial workshops, or 
    live plantfloor training.
  - Document Management: The Human Resources and Corporate Governance branches must 
    meticulously track and retain auditable records of all skilling events, hours, 
    and participants. This policy must be hosted publicly on the web domain.

4. CORPORATE SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY (CSR) POLICY
===============================================================================

4.1 LEGAL BOUNDS & STATUTORY DISQUALIFICATIONS
  - Normal Business Exclusion: Projects executed by the company in its normal course 
    of manufacturing operations are completely barred from being categorized as CSR.
  - Employee-Benefit Restrictions: Stated initiatives that primarily benefit only 
    the active employees of the company or their direct familial dependents will 
    not be classified as legitimate CSR under Section 135 of the Companies Act.
  - Political Prohibition: Direct or indirect funding, resource allocations, or contributions 
    provided to any political faction under Section 182 of the Act are strictly excluded.

4.2 APPROVED STRATEGIC CSR ACTIVITIES
  - Women's Empowerment & Education: Running specialized skill centers providing free education 
    to girls in English, computer literacy, and advanced technological vocational skills.
  - Charitable Healthcare Operations: Financing and managing free charitable dispensaries 
    and community health clinics to supply clinical treatments and medicine to underprivileged neighborhoods.
  - General Social Core Pillars: Supporting vocational training for the differently abled; 
    fostering gender equality; preserving local environmental biomes, flora, and fauna; 
    supporting historic preservation; and supporting state-sanctioned technology incubators.

4.3 GOVERNANCE, EXECUTION AND MONITORING
  - Committee Oversight: The formal Board-appointed CSR Committee reviews project scopes, 
    authorizes authorized personnel, and details precise capital disbursements.
  - Monitoring Framework: The Committee may assign a dedicated three-member 
    Internal Monitoring Group tasked with compiling continuous project field evaluations.
  - Project Appraisal Metrics: Projects are assessed against formal metrics including:
    * Technical project architecture soundness.
    * Line-item financial budget validation and matching fund security.
    * Time-chart milestone schedules.
    * Comprehensive, auditable project documentation.

5. ENVIRONMENT, SOCIAL, AND GOVERNANCE (ESG) & SUSTAINABILITY POLICY
===============================================================================

5.1 ENVIRONMENTAL FOOTPRINT & CARBON MITIGATION
  - ASL recognizes its moral obligations regarding environmental stewardship. The company 
    is dedicated to systematically tracking, measuring, and actively minimizing its 
    carbon footprint across all functional production mills, foundries, and administrative blocks.
  - Heavy focus is placed on enhancing energy efficiency throughout the melting capacity 
    (currently rated at 80,000 MT) and maintaining clean emissions controls.

5.2 QUALITY ASSURANCE & SECTORAL CREDIBILITY
  - Strict compliance with ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management Systems across all steel melting, 
    rolling, and cold-finishing facilities.
  - Strict adherence to International Automotive Task Force (IATF 16949:2016) standards to ensure 
    high-reliability, defect-free long product supply chains to safety-critical automotive lines globally.

5.3 FINANCIAL RISK & FOREX MANAGEMENT
  - To mitigate market volatility linked to international raw material procurements (which comprise 
    70%-75% of operational revenue), the company utilizes a structured forward contract facility 
    to hedge against foreign exchange fluctuations and protect consolidated corporate liquidity.

6. DIGITAL PRIVACY & INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
===============================================================================

6.1 DATA DEFINITION & COLLECTION BOUNDS
  - Scope: Governs processing of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) captured 
    via corporate portals, application tools, and digital catalog requests.
  - Collected Data Elements: Scope is restricted to voluntarily provided client/user metrics 
    including corporate email, name, company affiliation, contact numbers, and structural 
    business profiles.

6.2 INFORMATION UTILIZATION FRAMEWORK
  - Data collected is strictly used to optimize product information distribution, process catalog downloads, 
    fulfill customer support inquiries, and disseminate authorized company announcements.

6.3 INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION PROTOCOLS
  - Vulnerability Assessments: Digital portals undergo routine automated security scanning 
    and malware assessments to proactively detect and neutralize system vulnerabilities.
  - Tracking Controls: No persistent cookies are deployed for user behavioral tracking purposes. 
    Users retain complete programmatic authority to turn off cookies in local browser configurations 
    without losing basic operational site accessibility.
